Papers review

Machine learning-based meta-analysis of colorectal cancer and inflammatory bowel disease

Colorectal cancer is a major health concern (bowl cancer) of which the mechanisms are not yet known. It also states that while identifying biomarkers (anything biological that suggests abnormal activity) can improve CRC detection, treatment and prevention, they don't lead to satisfactory insights. This may be because of the prevalence of uni variate (single variable) analysis methods. Hence they use a machine-learning strategy to do multi variate analysis and pinpoint genes associated with CRC. They then tested the model with numerous datasets and present both previously and new genes that are linked to CRC. (Sardari & Usefi, 2023)

https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36709790/

lncRNA is shown to be associated with cancer metastatic events (the spreading of cancer into different parts of the body). This article uses a variational graph auto-encoder to try and find the associations between lncRNA and cancer metastasis. The paper claims that its the first computational model to find a link between the two and could be used to guide further research. (Zhu et al, 2023)

https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0031320323006647#section-cited-by

Using images from Spatial transcriptomics, it uses exemplars from previous windows in the tissue slide image to predict gene expressions (how the data in a gene is physically expressed, i.e. the function it performs). Its special because of the fact it uses exemplars from nearby windows to assist in its predictions. (Yang et al, 2024)
